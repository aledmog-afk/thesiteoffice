What is a RAMS and do I legally need one?

A RAMS (Method Statement + Risk Assessment) sets out how a task will be carried out safely. It identifies hazards, assesses the risk, and puts controls in place to protect operatives. Under the Management of Health & Safety at Work Regulations 1999 and CDM 2015, you are required to assess and manage risks on site. Most principal contractors and clients require a signed RAMS before work begins. Not having one — or having a poor one — leaves you exposed if there is ever an incident.
